SYNOPSIS

In the darkness of the night, the sky over Beacon Hills is illuminated by the presence of a full moon. Years have passed since the events depicted in the popular television series, Teen Wolf. Scott McCall, who has adapted to living as a normal human and working as a veterinarian, is suddenly thrown into action. An evil force is brewing, marked by the ominous full moon, and all sorts of supernatural creatures are being summoned. From banshees and hellhounds, to werecoyotes and kitsunes, the forces of darkness are converging towards a new source of power. Scott, the alpha werewolf, is the only one who can rally his old and new allies to face this grave threat.

WHAT TO KNOW

"Teen Wolf: The Movie" is a supernatural teenage drama feature film that was directed by the talented Australian filmmaker, Russell Mulchay. Known for his distinct style, which showcases fast-paced editing and dynamic camera movements, he has directed notable films such as "Resident Evil: Extinction," "Highlander," and "Razorback." This new film continues the story from the popular MTV series, "Teen Wolf," and features a screenplay written by Jeff Davis, who also wrote for the original show. The movie is distributed by Paramount+ and produced by MTV Entertainment Studios. The core cast from the original series, which was released in 2011, returns for this installment, including Tyler Posey as the central character Scott McCall and Crystal Reed as Allison Argent. The cast also includes Tyler Hoechlin, Holland Roden, Colton Haynes, and Shelley Henning. "Teen Wolf: The Movie" was made available on January 26, 2023, through Paramount+.
